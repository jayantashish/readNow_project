Sachin Tendulkar

Sachin Tendulkar - The "Master Blaster" and the "Little Master,".

SYNOPSIS

Sachin Tendulkar, often referred to as the "Master Blaster" and the "Little Master," is a retired Indian cricketer who is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of the sport. Born on April 24, 1973, in Mumbai, India, Tendulkar made his international debut for the Indian cricket team at the age of 16. Over his illustrious career spanning over two decades, he accumulated numerous records and accolades, becoming a symbol of cricketing excellence. Tendulkar's batting prowess was characterized by his impeccable technique, remarkable concentration, and an extensive array of shots. He held numerous records, including being the highest run-scorer in both Test and One Day International (ODI) formats until his retirement. Tendulkar's career was studded with memorable innings, including his hundred international centuries, a feat that remains unparalleled. Beyond his individual achievements, Tendulkar's contributions played a crucial role in shaping India's cricketing success on the global stage. He was part of India's World Cup-winning team in 2011, a victory that resonated deeply with cricket fans across the nation. Tendulkar's impact extended beyond the cricket field. His humility, dedication, and sportsmanship earned him respect and admiration worldwide. He received numerous awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, and has been involved in mentoring, charity work, and promoting the sport. Sachin Tendulkar's legacy as a cricketer, an icon, and a role model continues to inspire generations, leaving an indelible mark on the world of cricket and beyond.

EARLY LIFE

Tendulkar's early life was marked by his affinity for cricket, which would later lead him to become one of the greatest cricketers in the history of the sport. From a young age, Tendulkar showed a keen interest in playing cricket. He was introduced to the game by his elder brother, Ajit Tendulkar, who recognized Sachin's talent and encouraged him to pursue the sport seriously. Ajit would often take Sachin to the nearby Shivaji Park in Mumbai to practice and hone his skills. Sachin's talent became evident when he was just a child. His exceptional hand-eye coordination, focus, and technique set him apart from his peers. At the age of 11, he scored a century in a school cricket match, which caught the attention of the cricketing community. In 1984, Sachin's coach, Ramakant Achrekar, identified his potential and began coaching him rigorously. Achrekar's coaching played a crucial role in shaping Tendulkar's skills and temperament. He would place a one-rupee coin on the stumps, and if Tendulkar managed to bat throughout the session without getting dismissed, he would earn the coin. This practice helped develop Tendulkar's focus and determination. At the age of 16, Tendulkar made his debut for the Bombay (Mumbai) cricket team in domestic cricket, showcasing his remarkable talent. His performances in domestic cricket soon caught the attention of the national selectors, and in 1989, he made his debut for the Indian cricket team. Tendulkar's early life was marked by his unwavering dedication to cricket, supported by his family, particularly his brother Ajit and his coach Ramakant Achrekar. These formative years laid the foundation for his illustrious cricketing career, which would go on to inspire millions of cricket enthusiasts around the world.

Why "God of Cricket" ?

Sachin Tendulkar is often referred to as the "God of Cricket" due to his extraordinary talent, achievements, and the immense impact he had on the sport. This nickname is a testament to his iconic status in cricket and the reverence he commands among fans, players, and experts. Here are some reasons why he earned this title: Unmatched Records:- Tendulkar holds numerous records in international cricket, including being the highest run-scorer in both Test and One Day International (ODI) formats. His record-breaking achievements set him apart as an exceptional player. Longevity:- Tendulkar's career spanned over two decades, during which he maintained consistently high performance levels. His ability to adapt and excel across different eras of the game showcased his exceptional skills and dedication. Iconic Innings:- Tendulkar played several memorable and iconic innings that became etched in cricket history. His ability to perform under pressure and in crucial matches earned him the admiration of fans and experts alike. Global Fan Following:- Tendulkar's popularity transcends borders. He has a massive fan following not only in India but also across cricket-loving nations worldwide. His presence on the field attracted fans to cricket, contributing to the global growth of the sport. Humility and Sportsmanship:- Despite his extraordinary success, Tendulkar remained humble and grounded throughout his career. His sportsmanship, respect for opponents, and dedication to the game earned him the admiration of fellow players and fans. Inspiration:- Tendulkar's journey from a young boy with a dream to a cricket legend inspired countless aspiring cricketers. He became a role model for aspiring athletes, encouraging them to pursue their passion and excel. Cultural Impact:- Cricket is deeply ingrained in Indian culture, and Tendulkar's achievements made him a cultural icon. He became a symbol of national pride and unity, representing the hopes and aspirations of millions. Legacy:- Even after retirement, Tendulkar continues to contribute to cricket as a mentor, advisor, and philanthropist. His legacy extends beyond his playing years. While the title "God of Cricket" is a colloquial expression of Tendulkar's greatness, it reflects the profound impact he has had on the sport and the reverence he holds among cricket enthusiasts around the world.

Here's a brief about Sachin's life:

  • 1973- Born April 24, Mumbai.
  • 1988- Scores 100 not out in his first first-class match for Bombay against Gujarat in the Ranji Trophy becoming the youngest cricketer to score a century on his first-class debut, aged 15 years and 232 days.
  • 1989- Makes Test debut for India against Pakistan in Karachi at the age of 16.
  • December,1989- Makes ODI debut against Pakistan but is dismissed by Waqar Younis without scoring a run.
  • 1990- Scores maiden Test century against England at Old Trafford.
  • 1992- At the age of 19 he becomes the first overseas-born player to represent Yorkshire.
  • 1994 September 9- Claims his first ODI century against Sri Lanka in Colombo after 79 one-day matches.
  • 1996- Leading run scorer at World Cup played in India, Pakistan and Sri Lanka with a total of 523 runs.
  • August,1996- Takes on the India captaincy although suffers two relatively unsuccessful stints during a four-year period, winning only four Tests and 23 ODIs.
  • 1997- Named Wisden Cricketer of the Year.
  • 1998- Scores his first double century for Mumbai against Australia in the Brabourne Stadium.
  • November,1999- Involved in India’s highest run-scoring ODI partnership with Rahul Dravid as the pair put on 339 against New Zealand. In the same match, he records the highest individual ODI score in Indian history with an unbeaten 186.
  • 2000- Gives up the captaincy of India after the two-Test series with South Africa.
  • 2001- Given a suspended ban of one game in light of alleged ball tampering during a match against South Africa at St George’s Park, Port Elizabeth. The ban is later lifted by the International Cricket Council after an investigation.
  • 2003- Named the Player of the Tournament at the 2003 Cricket World Cup and scores 673 runs, the highest by any player in the tournament.
  • 2005- Overtakes Sunil Gavaskar’s record of the highest number of Test centuries by claiming his 35th against Sri Lanka in Delhi.
  • 2006- Overtakes Kapil Dev claiming the record for the highest amount of Test appearances for India with 135.
  • January 3,2007- Edges past Brian Lara’s world record of runs scored in Tests away from home with 5,751 runs.
  • June,2007- Becomes the first player to score over 15,000 ODI runs during a match against South Africa in Belfast.
  • March,2008- Plays a record-breaking 417th ODI match against Australia.
  • June,2008- Nominated as the ‘Icon Player’ of IPL franchise Mumbai Indians, signing a deal worth US Dollars 1,121,250 per season.
  • August,2008- Becomes just the third player in Test match history, and the first from India, to play 150 matches when he is selected in the third Test against Sri Lanka.
  • October 17,2008- Becomes the highest run scorer in Test cricket, passing Lara’s previous mark of 11,953, when he reaches 16 in the first innings of the second Test against Australia.
  • February 24,2010- Scores the first double century in one-day international history, hitting an unbeaten 200 against South Africa.
  • April,2010- Named player of IPL3 after finishing as the tournament’s top run-scorer.
  • August 3,2010- Becomes most capped player in Test history, making his 169th Test appearance in the third Test against Sri Lanka in Colombo to overtake former Australia captain Steve Waugh.
  • December 19,2010- Becomes first batsman to hit 50 Test centuries, achieving the feat against South Africa in Centurion.
  • March,2011- Hits two tournament centuries as co-hosts India win the World Cup.
  • November,2011- Becomes the first man to score 15,000 Test runs during Test series against West Indies.
  • March,2012- Scores his 100th international century in an Asia Cup match against Bangladesh, the first player ever to do so.
  • December 23,2012- Announces retirement from one-day international cricket.
  • October 10,2013- Announces he will retire from Test cricket after playing in his 200th Test against the West Indies in November.

The Legacy and Retirement

When India hosted 2011 World Cup, the country knew that it would be his last chance to lift the trophy. It would have been unfair to the game if its greatest icon never got the chance to lift it. The stage was set on 2nd April 2011, when India took on Sri Lanka in the World Cup final. Sachin Tendulkar had the chance to score his 100th International century to lift the World Cup at his home ground. But sports don’t have a script, Sachin got out for just 18 and it looked that his dream will never come true. This time, his teammates did not let him as Dhoni and Gambhir took India over the line as India won the World Cup. The team lifted Sachin Tendulkar on their shoulders. Virat Kohli said that Sachin Tendulkar had carried the burden of Indian cricket for so long so it justified them carrying Sachin on shoulders now. The nightmare came true, While the 100th century vs Bangladesh, made sure his name will be immortal, but it was a fact that his body was a mere mortal like us. When he announced his decision to retire in 2013, it became a national news. No matter how beautiful the story is, it has an end. Even this story had to end someday, Sachin retired on 14th November 2013. The nation was crying when Sachin Tendulkar was giving his farewell speech, he called it a day by showing his respect to the 22 yards which gave him a status of God. The crowd thanked him by chanting “Sachin Sachin…”

Some achievements of Sachin Tendulkar after the Retirement

  • Bharat Ratna Award:- Sachin Tendulkar, after his retirement, received India's highest civilian award, the Bharat Ratna, in 2014 for his outstanding contribution to cricket and sports.
  • Cricketing Mentor and Advisor:- After retirement, Tendulkar has been involved in mentoring and advising various cricket teams, including the Indian cricket team and MI in the Indian Premier League (IPL).
  • Cricketing Records:- Even after retirement, Tendulkar's numerous cricketing records and statistics continue to stand as some of the most remarkable achievements in the history of the sport.
  • Writing Autobiography:- Tendulkar authored his autobiography, "Playing It My Way," which became a bestseller and offered insights into his cricketing journey and personal experiences.
  • Philanthropy and Charity Work:- Tendulkar has been actively involved in various philanthropic and charity initiatives, supporting causes related to education, healthcare, and underprivileged children.
  • Sports Icon:- Sachin Tendulkar remains a global sports icon and ambassador for cricket. He has played a role in promoting the sport and encouraging young talent.
